## 1. The Market Failure: The "Average Price" Fallacy
Industrial energy consumers in India have historically relied on long-term Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) and average cost-of-service metrics. However, as the grid transitions to 500GW of renewables, the "Average" price has become a dangerous fiction.

**The Problem**: A factory might have a ₹5/kWh average cost, but if the Real-Time Market (RTM) spikes to ₹12/kWh for 4 hours during their peak shift, the unplanned surcharge can erase 15-20% of their monthly operational margin.
